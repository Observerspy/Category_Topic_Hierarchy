{{Politics of Poland}}
The popular '''election of the [[President of Poland]]''' was held on 20 June 2010. As no candidate received a majority of votes in the first round, a second round was held on 4 July 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/8633981.stm |title=Poland's snap presidential elections set for June 20 |publisher=BBC News |date=21 April 2010 |accessdate=21 June 2010}}</ref> [[Bronisław Komorowski]], candidate of [[Civic Platform]], defeated [[Jarosław Kaczyński]], candidate of [[Law and Justice]]. The [[Financial crisis of 2007–2010|global financial crisis]], [[2010 Central European floods|flooding in Poland]] and the [[2010 Polish Air Force Tu-154 crash|Smolensk disaster]] were the main themes in the last months of the election campaign.

==Background==
After President [[Lech Kaczyński]]'s death in [[2010 Polish Air Force Tu-154 crash|a plane crash]] on 10 April 2010, the Constitution required the [[Marshal of the Sejm]] to declare the date within two weeks, with the election to take place on a weekend within the following 60 days, i.e. 20 June at the latest. On 21 April, the Marshal, [[Bronisław Komorowski]], announced the election date as 20 June 2010.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.businessweek.com/news/2010-04-14/poland-may-hold-early-election-on-june-20-napieralski-says.html |title=Poland May Hold Election June 20, Napieralski Says (Update1) |publisher=BusinessWeek |date=14 April 2010 |accessdate=21 June 2010}}</ref> Candidates were required to register by 26 April 2010 (with 1,000 signatures of voters in support) and submit 100,000 signatures by 6 May 2010.<ref>{{cite news|last=Sobczyk |first=Marcin |url=http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748704133804575197321643319484.html?mod=WSJ_latestheadlines |title=Polish Election Set for June 20 - WSJ.com |publisher=Online.wsj.com |date=21 April 2010 |accessdate=21 June 2010}}</ref>

Originally, Kaczyński was up for re-election between 19 September and 3 October; the exact date would have been announced between 23 May and 23 June, before the end of his first five-year term of office.<ref>[http://www.sejm.gov.pl/prawo/konst/angielski/kon1.htm#5 Constitution of Poland], Article 128</ref>

Two candidates for the election died in the crash. Incumbent Lech Kaczyński was nominated as the [[Law and Justice]] party candidate (he had yet to accept the nomination, but it was widely believed that he would do so), and [[Jerzy Szmajdziński]] was to have run for the [[Democratic Left Alliance]].<ref>{{cite web | title = Jerzy Szmajdziński - kandydat SLD na prezydenta | publisher = wyborcza.pl | date = 20 December 2009 | url = http://wyborcza.pl/1,75478,7385246,Jerzy_Szmajdzinski___kandydat_SLD_na_prezydenta.html | accessdate = 22 December 2009 |language=pl}}</ref>

[[Polish Senate by-elections, 2010|Senate by-elections]] to fill the three seats whose senators died in the crash – [[Krystyna Bochenek]] (PO), [[Janina Fetlińska]] (PiS) and [[Stanisław Zając]] (PiS, himself elected in a by-election on 22 June 2008 to replace [[Andrzej Mazurkiewicz]]) – were held on the same day.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.polishmarket.com.pl/document/:22912?p=%2FEconomic+Monitor%2F |title=Polish Market Online .:. Polish Market Online .:. What’s next for Poland? |publisher=Polishmarket.com.pl |date=12 April 2010 |accessdate=21 June 2010}}</ref>

== Candidates ==
In total, 23 candidates (all of them men) registered with the Polish National Electoral Commission on, or just following, the 26 April deadline. 17 of the 23 candidacies were accepted by the commission, whilst the remaining 6 were rejected because they had not gathered the required 1000 signatures.

Only ten candidates collected the required 100,000 signatures and were eligible to run for President.<ref>{{cite web|author=jagor, zsz |url=http://wiadomosci.gazeta.pl/Wiadomosci/1,80271,7851073,Wybory_2010__Dziewieciu_kandydatom_na_prezydenta_udalo.html |title=Wybory 2010. Dziesięciu kandydatom na prezydenta udało się zebrać 100 tys. podpisów |publisher=Wiadomosci.gazeta.pl |date= |accessdate=21 June 2010|language=pl}}</ref>

== Results ==

===First round===
There were 10 candidates in the first round of voting. [[Bronisław Komorowski]] of [[Civic Platform]] received 41.5% and [[Jarosław Kaczyński]] of [[Law and Justice]] received 36.5%, causing a second round of voting between the two. The other eight candidates were eliminated.<ref name="news.bbc.co.uk">{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/world/europe/10503555.stm|title=Bronislaw Komorowski declared president of Poland|date=5 July 2010|work=[[BBC News]]|publisher=[[British Broadcasting Corporation]]|accessdate=5 July 2010}}</ref>

===Second round===
[[File:Voting card Polish presidential elections 2010 Poznan.jpg|thumb|220px|Voting card in 2nd Round]]
Soon after exit polls were released in the second round, [[Jarosław Kaczyński]] conceded that he had been defeated. [[Bronisław Komorowski]] appeared to tentatively claim victory, stating: "Tonight we will open a small bottle of champagne and tomorrow we will open a big bottle."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/10503555.stm|title=Kaczynski accepts Polish election defeat|date=4 July 2010|work=[[BBC News]]|publisher=[[British Broadcasting Corporation]]|accessdate=4 July 2010}}</ref> The exit polls put Komorowski ahead of Kaczyński by 53% to 47%.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2010/07/05/2944462.htm?section=world|title=Liberal beats bereaved twin in Polish vote|date=5 July 2010|work=[[ABC News (Australia)|ABC News]]|publisher=[[Australian Broadcasting Corporation]]|accessdate=4 July 2010}}</ref> On the following day, Komorowski was declared the winner of the election.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.google.com/hostednews/ap/article/ALeqM5ir8JleEyG0Yo_1nhoC5R225rcGjAD9GP26B80|title=Komorowski win strengthens Polish government|last=Gera|first=Vanessa|date=5 July 2010|work=[[Associated Press]]|accessdate=5 July 2010}}</ref> The final result put Komorowski with 53.01% of the vote, and Kaczyński with 46.99%.<ref name="news.bbc.co.uk"/>
{{wikinews|Bronislaw Komorowski wins Polish presidency in run-off}}

Komorowski's win resulted in Civic Platform holding both the Presidency and the government (under Prime Minister [[Donald Tusk]]).<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/poland/7873488/Polands-liberals-dominate-after-dead-presidents-twin-defeated.html|title=Poland's liberals dominate after dead president's twin defeated|date=5 July 2010|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|publisher=Telegraph Media Group|accessdate=5 July 2010 | location=London | first=Our | last=Foreign}}</ref> Correspondents in US and British business newspapers suggested that Komorowski's win would mean closer engagement with the [[European Union]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748703620604575348772126843284.html?mod=googlenews_wsj|title=Poland Vote Strengthens European Ties|last=Fairclough|first=Gordon|date=5 July 2010|work=[[The Wall Street Journal]]|publisher=[[Dow Jones & Company]]|accessdate=5 July 2010}}</ref> and such domestic economic reforms as deficit reduction.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/0/12d2922a-8859-11df-aade-00144feabdc0.html|title=Poland poised for vital reforms|last=Buckley|first=Neil|date=5 July 2010|work=[[Financial Times]]|publisher=[[Pearson PLC]]|accessdate=5 July 2010}}</ref>
