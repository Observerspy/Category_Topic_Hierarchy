A '''general election''' was held in [[Mexico]] on Sunday, July 1, 2012. Voters went to the polls to elect, on the federal level:
* A new [[president of Mexico|President of the Republic]] to serve a six-year term, replacing former President [[Felipe Calderón]]. The current [[Constitution of Mexico]] prohibits any type of presidential re-election.
* 500 members (300 by the [[first-past-the-post]] system and 200 by [[proportional representation]]) to serve for a three-year term in the [[Chamber of Deputies of Mexico|Chamber of Deputies]].
* 128 members (three per [[States of Mexico|state]] by first-past-the-post and 32 by proportional representation from [[List of political parties in Mexico|national party]] lists) to serve six-year terms in the [[Senate of Mexico|Mexican Senate]]. In each state, two first-past-the-post seats are allocated to the party with the largest share of the vote, and the remaining seat is given to the first runner-up.

Several local ballots were held on the same day, notably:

* The election of a new [[Head of Government of the Federal District|Head of Government]] and new [[Legislative Assembly of the Federal District|Legislative Assembly]] of the [[Mexican Federal District|Federal District]].
** [[Federal District of Mexico head of government election, 2012|2012 Federal District head of government election]]
* Gubernatorial elections in six [[States of Mexico|states]]
** [[Chiapas gubernatorial election, 2012|Chiapas gubernatorial election]] - held by PAN/PRD before the election
** [[Guanajuato gubernatorial election, 2012|Guanajuato gubernatorial election]] - held by PAN before the election
** [[Jalisco gubernatorial election, 2012|Jalisco gubernatorial election]] - held by PAN before the election
** [[Morelos gubernatorial election, 2012|Morelos gubernatorial election]] - held by PAN before the election
** [[Tabasco gubernatorial election, 2012|Tabasco gubernatorial election]] - held by PRI before the election
** [[Yucatán gubernatorial election, 2012|Yucatán gubernatorial election]] - held by PRI before the election

* Municipal and local congressional elections in those six states and in [[Campeche]], [[Colima]], [[Nuevo León]], [[Querétaro]], [[Sonora]], and [[San Luis Potosí]].

==Electoral reform==
In December 2009, president Felipe Calderón sent a bill to Congress aimed at reducing the number of legislators in both chambers and another mechanism for the presidential election which have not yet been passed. If approved, the following reforms will be implemented:
* Second round voting in case no presidential candidate receives an absolute majority of the votes.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2010/07/05/us-mexico-elections-idUSTRE6643PF20100705 | work=Reuters | title=Mexico state votes signal tough 2012 election race | date=July 5, 2010}}</ref><ref>http://www.electionguide.org/country.php?ID=140</ref>
* 96 Senators of the Republic, 3 for each state, elected by plurality for a period of six years, renewable once.
* 400 Federal Deputies (240 by first-past-the-post and 160 by proportional representation) elected for a period of three years with possibility of reelection.

==Allegations of media bias and ''Yo Soy 132'' student protests==
{{Main|Yo Soy 132}}
Mass protests have taken place in Mexico City against alleged bias towards PRI and Peña Nieto in the print and television media, particularly [[Televisa]].<ref>http://www.aljazeera.com/news/americas/2012/05/201252492924104136.html</ref>

The movement ''Yo Soy 132'' ("I am 132") formed in response to this perceived bias, with initial focus on Peña Nieto as the flagship of "corruption, tyranny and authoritarianism".<ref>http://www.razon.com.mx/spip.php?article124227</ref> On May 11, 2012, Peña Nieto visited [[Universidad Iberoamericana]] and was received with scorn.<ref>http://www.sexenio.com.mx/articulo.php?id=15175</ref> Both printed and televised media reported this as a minor mishap, called the students intolerant, and claimed that they had been paid by leftist organizations. In response, 131 students identified themselves on a [[YouTube]] video by their university IDs and stated that that their actions were independent. This caused a ripple effect as many [[Twitter|tweeted]] "I'm the 132nd student" in solidarity. Mass protests organized by public and private university students then took place across the country. The movement successfully demanded that, unlike the first debate, the second presidential debate be broadcast on national television, and has proposed a third debate to cover a broader scope of issues.<ref>http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/notas/850042.html</ref><ref>http://www.elinfluyente.com/tv-azteca-tambien-cede-el-debate-ira-por-canal-13/</ref>

===Recount===
Following the elections, López Obrador demanded a full recount, claiming there had been widespread irregularities.<ref name=BBC>[http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-18717146 Mexico faces partial recount in presidential election] BBC News, 5 July 2012</ref> The Federal Electoral Institute subsequently announced that there would be a partial recount, with presidential ballots from 78,012 of the 143,132 polling stations to be recounted, whilst ballots for the Congressional elections would be re-examined at two-thirds of polling stations.<ref name=BBC/> On July 6 after a partial recount of approximately 50% of the votes, the [[Federal Electoral Institute]] declared they had found anomalies but that Nieto still had majority and was confirmed as the winner with 38.2% of the popular vote.<ref>{{cite news|title=Mexico's Pena Nieto's presidential victory confirmed|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/18736492|accessdate=6 July 2012 | work=BBC News|date=July 6, 2012}}BBC News</ref> which officially has until 6 September to announce a winner.<ref name=BBC/>

===Request to Invalidate Election===
On July 12, López Obrador presented his formal complaint to invalidate the election to the [[TEPJF|Electoral Tribunal of the Federal Judiciary]] on grounds of violation of constitutional article 41 which states that the elections must be free and authentic and with equal benefits for all competing parties.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://mexico.cnn.com/nacional/2012/07/13/violaciones-al-articulo-41-de-la-constitucion | work=CNN | title=Lo más popular | date=July 13, 2012}}</ref> Alleging that the Mexican media had treated them with inequality in relation to Peña Nieto and presenting the numerous evidence of paraphernalia used to buy votes for the PRI as well as pre-marked ballots and notarized witness statements of people who sold their votes to the PRI.<ref>http://aristeguinoticias.com/pena-nieto-fue-el-candidato-de-las-televisoras-amlo/</ref> The complaint also pointed towards the PRI's campaign going over budget an alleged 1000% over the established allowed budget by the Federal Electoral Institute which is of 336 million Mexican pesos.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://mexico.cnn.com/nacional/2012/07/13/rebase-de-tope-de-gasto-de-campana | work=CNN | title=Lo más popular | date=July 13, 2012}}</ref> On July 18 López Obrador accused Peña Nieto of using illicit funds and money laundering to finance his campaign.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2012/jul/19/lopez-obrador-election-money-laundering | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Jo | last=Tuckman | title=Mexico presidential runner-up alleges money laundering in election | date=July 19, 2012}}</ref> After presenting new audio evidence regarding the PRI's use of Monex cards, López Obrador commented that it would be better if the Electoral Tribunal invalidated the election and move in an [[interim]] President than to violate the constitution and acting in an "[[anti-democratic]]" way. He said that if the Electoral Tribunal does not invalidate the election, Mexico will be governed by a "gang of evildoers".<ref>http://aristeguinoticias.com/es-preferible-tener-presidente-interino-amlo/</ref>
On 30 August 2012, the [[TEPJF]], Mexico's highest election-law court rejected the allegations of fraud after they concluded that there was "insufficient evidence of wrongdoing."<ref>{{cite news|title=UPDATE 2-Mexico electoral judges reject challenge to Pena Nieto victory|url=http://in.reuters.com/article/2012/08/31/mexico-election-tribunal-idINL2E8JV0BF20120831|accessdate=31 August 2012|newspaper=[[Reuters]]|date=31 August 2012}}</ref>

==Post-electoral protests and claims of fraud==
After the preliminary results of the [[Federal Electoral Institute]] announced Enrique Peña Nieto as virtual [[President-elect]], several student protests led by the youth movement [[Yo Soy 132]] and independent citizen movements, have broken out throughout the country claiming the forced imposition of a President and [[electoral fraud]],<ref>http://english.ntdtv.com/ntdtv_en/news_northamerica/2012-07-04/youths-protest-mexican-president-elect-pena-nieto.html</ref><ref>http://www.critica.com.mx/vernoticias.php?artid=44364&mas=5</ref><ref>http://www.sobrepapel.com.mx/vernoticias.php?artids=4453&categoria=10</ref><ref>http://www.frontera.info/EdicionEnLinea/Notas/Noticias/04072012/604835.aspx</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-latin-america-18700953 | work=BBC News | title=No early end to Mexico's election stand-off | date=July 4, 2012}}</ref><ref>http://www.elmundo.es/america/2012/07/05/mexico/1341513712.html</ref> as evidence of an alleged [[electoral fraud|fraud]] has surfaced and pointed towards the PRI buying votes by providing voting citizens with store [[credit card]]s of Mexican supermarket chain [[Soriana]].<ref>http://www.bbc.co.uk/mundo/noticias/2012/07/120704_mexico_pena_nieto_entrevista_ao.shtml</ref><ref>http://www.sandiegored.com/noticias/26982/Soriana-gate-las-versiones-de-los-hechos/</ref> Mexico's Federal Electoral Institute (IFE) confirmed on January 2013 that Peña Nieto's party spent $5.2 million in electronic cards throughout the whole campaign. Opposition parties complained that this represented a form of illicit funding, but the IFE claimed the contrary. The PRI party claimed that the electronic cards were intended for party personnel, but this method rose suspicions because some of the money was transacted through "several shadowy companies instead of being disbursed directly from party coffers."<ref name=monexsan>{{cite news|title=Mexico: ruling party used $5.2 million cash cards|url=http://www.mysanantonio.com/news/world/article/Mexico-ruling-party-used-5-2-million-cash-cards-4221504.php|accessdate=27 January 2013|newspaper=[[San Antonio Express-News]]|date=24 January 2013|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6DzkTqrpy|archivedate=27 January 2013|deadurl=no}}</ref> This also increased the suspicions that the PRI had received illegal donations from corporations (given that this move is prohibited under law). The IFE stated on 24 January 2013 and ruled by 5-4 votes that the fundings were not violating the law, but opposition parties and critics believe that the IFE did not thoroughly investigate the origins of the money.<ref name=monexsan/>

Further alleged evidence arose as pictures of ballots already marked in favor of the PRI, with the logo of the party printed over the marking, have been shared widely over online social networks,<ref>http://sdpnoticias.com/nota/348518/Distribuyen_en_redes_imagen_de_boleta_tachada_a_favor_del_PRI_por_debajo_del_logo_impreso</ref> and there have also been numerous videos and photos of that show the irregularities between local ballot boxes and the official result of those ballot boxes.<ref>http://www.el5antuario.org/2012/07/algunas-sabanas-electorales-para.html</ref> More allegations appeared as videos showing protection of local police patrols protecting supposed "Mapaches". Following a request from Andrés Manuel López Obrador, the [[Federal Electoral Institute]] agreed to recount more than half of the ballots cast in the presidential election. It later reconfirmed the original result.<ref name="Economist 14July">{{cite news |title=Counted out: López Obrador, sore loser |publisher=The Economist |date=14 July 2012 |url=http://www.economist.com/node/21558611}}</ref> The result was endorsed by [[Barack Obama]], the [[president of the United States]], and by the [[Venezuela]]n president [[Hugo Chávez]], who backed López Obrador in a similar dispute in 2006.<ref name="Economist 14July"/>

On July 7, a national protest in opposition of Enrique Peña Nieto was organized through online social networks, and demonstrations occurred in several cities. The protest in [[Mexico City]] was billed as a "mega-march", but the number of demonstrators equalled only around half the number which attended anti-PRI demonstrations held prior to the election.<ref name="Economist 14July"/> Statements from the [[Yo Soy 132]] student movement formally uninvolved themselves from the protest cautioning against violent results; alleging that it may have been organized by a similarly named movement linked to the [[Partido Revolucionario Institucional|PRI]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://mexico.cnn.com/nacional/2012/07/06/convocan-en-redes-a-marcha-anti-pena-yosoy132-se-deslinda | work=CNN | title=Convocan en redes a marcha anti-Peña; #Yosoy132 se deslinda - México: Voto 2012 - Nacional - CNNMexico.com | date=July 6, 2012}}</ref> Despite the statement the protests effectively took place in 37 cities in [[Mexico]], [[Canada]], [[The United States]], and [[Europe]]<ref>http://www.elmanana.com.mx/notas.asp?id=293001</ref> with no incidents of violence or known involvement of the PRI. Though in [[Xalapa, Veracruz]] a man identified as Juan Pablo Frianzoni, presumed member of the youth PRI group "Juventud Dinamica"; threw chairs at the protesters and then pointed a handgun at them from his balcony. Frianzoni was then apprehended by Police officers who were standing by the protest.<ref>http://sdpnoticias.com/nota/348750/Amenaza_sujeto_armado_a_manifestantes_de_MegaMarcha_en_Verazcruz</ref> [[Televisa]] did not broadcast the National protests, and instead presented a live broadcast of [[Eugenio Derbez]] and [[Alessandra Rosaldo]]'s wedding which was interrupted due to "technical difficulties" when loud protest chants became audible outside of the event.<ref>http://sdpnoticias.com/nota/348752/Cortan_transmision_de_boda_de_Eugenio_Derbez_en_Televisa_tras_protestas_de_MegaMarcha</ref> Derbez however stated that he was in support of the protests, and though he regrets them happening on the day of his wedding; he said he will cherish the memory.<ref>http://sdpnoticias.com/nota/348757/Eugenio_Derbez_toma_con_humor_protestas_en_boda_muestra_apoyo_a_YoSoy132_con_foto</ref>

As of July 15<ref>http://www.milenio.com/cdb/doc/noticias2011/e4e7fe9f7a613accf953b326bc1dc90a</ref> protests and further acts of civil resistance against Peña Nieto and Televisa continue around the country.<ref>http://www.rnw.nl/espanol/bulletin/yosoy132-y-otros-colectivos-se-movilizar%C3%A1n-contra-regreso-del-pri-en-m%C3%A9xico-0</ref> On July 27 protesters set up a 24 hour blockade around the main [[Televisa]] studios in [[Chapultepec]], [[Mexico City]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2012/jul/27/mexican-blockade-televisa-election-scandal | location=London | work=The Guardian | title=Mexican demonstrators blockade Televisa studios over election scandal | date=July 27, 2012}}</ref>
On August 30 several protesters gathered outside the offices of the [[TEPJF|Electoral Tribunal of the Federal Judiciary]] as the Magistrates declared that the claims made by the left-wing coalition were "unfounded" and were therefore rejected.<ref>http://www.razon.com.mx/spip.php?article136863</ref>

On December 1, 2012, as Peña Nieto was being sworn in as President, protesters rioted outside of the national palace and clashed with [[Mexican Federal Police|Federal Police]] forces while vandalizing hotel structures and setting fires around Mexico City. More than 90 protesters were arrested and several were injured. Mexico City Mayor [[Marcelo Ebrard]] blamed anarchist groups for causing the violent outcomes.<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2012/12/02/world/americas/enrique-pena-nieto-takes-office-as-mexicos-president.html?_r=1& | work=The New York Times | first1=Randal C. | last1=Archibold | first2=Karla | last2=Zabludovsky | title=Enrique Peña Nieto Takes Office as Mexico's President | date=December 1, 2012}}</ref>
